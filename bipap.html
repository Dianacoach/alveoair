BiPAP (Bilevel Positive Airway Pressure)

A BiPAP machine is a non-invasive device that helps support your breathing by delivering two different levels of air pressure—higher during inhalation and lower during exhalation.

This difference in pressure makes it easier to breathe in and, just as important, easier to breathe out. It is commonly used for conditions such as COPD, sleep apnea, and other situations where breathing becomes difficult or ineffective.

Unlike CPAP, which provides constant pressure, BiPAP assists with ventilation by improving the movement of air in and out of the lungs. This can reduce the work of breathing and help prevent fatigue of the respiratory muscles.

How BiPAP Can Help

When CPAP Is Not Tolerated

Some people have difficulty exhaling against continuous pressure. BiPAP reduces that resistance by lowering pressure during exhalation, making breathing more comfortable and sustainable.

Support During Respiratory Stress

In conditions such as COPD flare-ups, infections, or early pneumonia, BiPAP can help stabilize breathing and may reduce the need for hospital-based interventions.

Improved Ventilation

By creating two pressure levels, BiPAP increases airflow and improves ventilation, helping remove carbon dioxide more effectively than CPAP alone.

Mask Selection & Fit

Choosing the right mask (full face, nasal, or under-nose) is essential for comfort, leak prevention, and effective therapy.
