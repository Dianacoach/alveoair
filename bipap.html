BIPAP

A BiPAP (Bilevel Positive Airway Pressure) machine is a device that helps you breathe by delivering two levels of air pressure—higher when you inhale and lower when you exhale. It is used to support people with breathing difficulties, such as sleep apnea, COPD, or respiratory weakness, making breathing easier and more effective.

BIPAP a posible prevention to Respiratory Failure

Unable to tolerate CPAP

People with poor lung compliance are more likely to tolerate BIPAP over CPAP, since their exhalation is poor and what they don't need is more resistance to their exhalation..

BIPAP step to prevent invasive ventilation(intubation)

Severe COPD patients are very likely to visit hospital and being intubated or placed on BIPAP. People with compromised lungs, a bad cold, flu or the begining of pneumonia can be a direct trip to a hospital just to be placed on BIPAP or intubated until they complete taking their antibiotics treatment

Why BIPAP could be the right Choise

The BIPAP is used with the idea of ventilation more than oxygenation by creating two pressures and so, creating an increased volume of air movement something that CPAP can't do.
