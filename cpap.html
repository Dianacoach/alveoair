CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure)

A CPAP machine delivers a constant and steady stream of air pressure to keep your airways open during breathing.

It is most commonly used to treat obstructive sleep apnea, a condition where the airway repeatedly collapses during sleep. By keeping the airway open, CPAP improves oxygen levels, reduces interruptions in breathing, and helps restore normal sleep patterns.

When used consistently, CPAP can significantly improve energy levels, reduce strain on the heart, and enhance overall quality of life.

Why CPAP Might Be the Right Option for You

Sleep Apnea & Loud Snoring

Repeated snoring, gasping, or waking up suddenly during sleep are signs of airway collapse. CPAP keeps the airway open, allowing continuous and restful breathing throughout the night.

Better Sleep & Energy

Waking up exhausted, even after a full night’s sleep, is often linked to poor oxygenation. CPAP helps restore normal sleep cycles, improving energy, focus, and overall well-being.

Breathing Support at Night

CPAP can help reduce breathing effort during sleep and improve oxygen levels, especially in people with underlying respiratory or cardiac conditions.

Mask Options & Comfort

Choosing the right mask (nasal, full face, or under-nose) is key to comfort and effectiveness. Proper fit helps prevent leaks and improves tolerance.

Common Problems & Solutions

Issues like dryness, discomfort, air leaks, or removing the mask during sleep are common. These can usually be corrected with small adjustments.

Getting Used to CPAP

Adapting to CPAP takes time. With the right approach, most people can become comfortable and benefit fully from the therapy.
