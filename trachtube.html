Tracheostomy Tube Guidance

A tracheostomy tube (trach tube) is a small medical device placed through an opening in the neck into the trachea (windpipe) to help a person breathe.

It allows air to go directly into the lungs when normal breathing through the nose and mouth is difficult, blocked, or unsafe. Tracheostomy tubes are commonly used for long-term respiratory support, airway protection, secretion management, or when assistance from a ventilator is required.

Proper care, cleaning, and understanding of the equipment are essential to prevent complications and ensure comfort and safety.
